A Conceptual Model for Spacetime Based on Temporal Disagreement

Author: Jason Morbin | Date: November 8, 2025

1. Abstract

I propose a conceptual model where reality is a temporal "space." Time is matter's true motion at $c$ along a unique temporal axis, while observed spatial motion is a gyroscopic precession of that true motion. Temporal axes point "inward" (the unperceivable future) and "outward" (the perceivable past). "Space" is our name for the relative directions and positions of this "outward" motion. This model provides a new "why" for the mathematics of Special Relativity, thermodynamics, and quantum uncertainty.

2. Core Axioms

  1. Motion: All matter travels at a constant velocity $c$ (the speed of time) along a unique "temporal axis."
  2. Perception: We cannot perceive our own "inward" (future) direction of travel. We perceive only the "outward" (past) illumination of all other temporal axes. This relative perception of past temporal positions is what we call "3D Space."
  3. Relativity: Observed spatial velocity ($v$) is a direct geometric consequence of the "disagreement angle" (α) between two temporal axes, given by the relation: sin(α) = $v/c$.
  4. The No-Rest Barrier: A state of perfect relative rest (α = 0) is impossible, as no two distinct particles can share an identical temporal axis.

3. Mathematical Validation

This conceptual model is not speculation; its core axiom (sin(α) = $v/c$) is a valid trigonometric formulation that is mathematically identical to the established framework of Special Relativity.

From this single geometric principle, the entire framework can be derived:

  • The Lorentz Factor: γ = sec(α) (or 1/cos(α))
  • The Energy-Momentum Relation: E² = E₀² + (pc)²
  • The Lorentz Transformations: The full transformations for $x'$ and $t'$

4. Unifying Hypotheses (The Capstone)

This model suggests a unified geometric origin for force, thermodynamics, and quantum mechanics.

  • On Force & Thermodynamics: Force is the interaction between temporal axes. Matter is a geometric collection of objects bound by their mutual "disagreement" (α). The magnitude of this internal disagreement within a stable, bound structure is what we measure as temperature.
  • On Quantum Uncertainty: The "no-rest" barrier (Axiom 4) is a direct consequence of the Uncertainty Principle. I hypothesize a geometric link: tan(α) = √(2X + X²), where X = (ΔE * Δt) / ħ. Because X can never be zero, α can never be zero. The fundamental uncertainty of quantum mechanics is the geometric source of the "no-rest" barrier.
