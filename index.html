Flux Theory

A Conceptual Model of Emergent Spacetime from Temporal Dynamics

A Conceptual Model for Spacetime Based on Temporal Disagreement

Author: Jason Morbin | Date: November 8, 2025

1. Introduction: The Central Thesis

Flux Theory re-contextualizes physical reality by positing that time, not space, is the fundamental stage of existence. Our perceived 3D spatial universe is a geometric and perceptual emergence, a "projection" from a more fundamental, multi-dimensional temporal reality[cite: 1, 2, 3]. This model seeks to provide a deeper, mechanistic "why" for the laws of relativity, the nature of mass, and the arrow of time, grounding them in the dynamics of information and thermodynamics[cite: 3, 5].

2. The Foundational Pillars

The theory rests on four interdependent principles that describe the origin and governance of reality:

  1. The Thermodynamic Field (TDF): The ultimate substrate of reality is a singular, temporally stationary field of potential information existing in a state of fundamental disequilibrium. It possesses an intrinsic "Relational Fabric" that enables entanglement and structure[cite: 3].
  2. Quantum Uncertainty: An intrinsic dynamic of the TDF, Uncertainty is the engine of novelty, spontaneously generating "Fluctuations" from the TDF's potential[cite: 3].
  3. Relational Actualization: Potential Fluctuations become "Actualized Information" (matter, energy) only through Relational Definition, a process mediated by Entanglement. Existence is fundamentally relational[cite: 3].
  4. The Thermodynamic Imperative (Flux): All processes are governed by a universal drive toward TDF equilibrium (Flux Irreversibility). This defines the arrow of time and compels all interactions, as "Forces" are the TDF's mechanisms to resolve thermodynamic violations[cite: 3, 5].

3. The Core Mechanism: Universal Temporal Dynamics

The "how" of the theory is its model of temporal kinematics. This framework supersedes earlier models (like "spacetime unfolding") and provides a direct derivation for relativity.

3.1. Universal c-Progression

The model posits a fundamental distinction between massive and massless entities:

  • Massive Particles (Matter): All actualized matter (e.g., electrons, quarks) exists in a state of constant, unidirectional temporal motion at an invariant speed c along a unique, individual "Temporal Axis," relative to the stationary TDF[cite: 3, 2]. This is its primary mode of existence.
  • Massless Phenomena (Light): Massless mediators (like photonic potential) are not particles *traveling* at c. They are **stationary potentials** within the TDF[cite: 3]. We *perceive* light as traveling at c because we, the observers, are progressing at c *past* these stationary potentials[cite: 3]. This distinction is the theory's explanation for the invariance of the speed of light.

3.2. Mass as Temporal Offset

If all matter moves at c, what is mass? Mass is **Temporal Offset**[cite: 3]. A massive particle persists by creating a "Temporal Offset Volume" (or "Persistence Pyramid")—a "local now" that is temporally out-of-sync (progressing slower) with the universal "Objective Now." The magnitude of this temporal depth/volume, maintained by internal coherence, *is* its rest mass (m₀)[cite: 3].

3.3. Emergent Relativity & The Temporo-Gyroscopic Model

This is the capstone of the temporal model, explaining spatial motion and relativity as a gyroscopic effect[cite: 3]:

  • The Temporal Gyroscope: Each massive particle is a "temporal gyroscope" with three fundamental (temporal) spin axes.
  • Primary T-Axis (Mass & Gravity): The gyroscope has an inherent, baseline spin (ωT) around its primary Temporal Axis. This spin is proportional to its **rest mass (m₀)**[cite: 3]. This axis also defines the primary "in/out" **gravitational dimension**. Gravity is the energy cost of moving against the "temporal vortex" or "spinning bubble" created by another object's mass-spin[cite: 3].
  • Secondary X, Y-Axes (Spatial Motion & Relativistic Mass): At rest, the other two internal axes (ωX, ωY) are inactive. When an external "FEN force" (a torque) is applied, energy is added, activating these spins. This energy:
    1. Is stored in the ωX, ωY spin modes, accounting for the **relativistic mass increase** (m = γm₀)[cite: 3].
    2. Simultaneously generates the **spatial velocity vector (v_spatial)**[cite: 3]. The two "precessional" spatial dimensions emerge from the dynamics of these two axes[cite: 3].
  • The Geometric Partition: This system *inherently* conserves the total linear velocity at c. The particle's total motion c is now "tilted" at a temporal divergence angle (α) relative to the observer. This motion is partitioned into two components:
    • v_time (progress along the observer's temporal axis)
    • v_spatial (the emergent spatial velocity)
    ...such that the Pythagorean relationship c² = v_time² + v_spatial² always holds. This geometry is the fundamental origin of time dilation[cite: 3, 2].

4. Mathematical Formalism (Current State)

The gyroscopic model's geometry provides a direct path to the core mathematics of Special Relativity, deriving them from temporal principles rather than postulating them. The "Temporal Divergence Angle" (α) is the key.

Kinematic Ratios[cite: 2]:

  • Temporal Projection Factor: cos(α) = v_time / c = 1/γ
    (This is the measure of time dilation.)
  • Spatial Projection Factor: sin(α) = v_spatial / c
    (This is the beta factor, v/c.)
  • Kinematic Divergence Factor: tan(α) = v_spatial / v_time
    (This is the ratio of spatial to temporal motion.)

Core Equations:

  • Velocity Partition: c² = v_time² + v_spatial²
    (The foundational partition of the invariant c-speed motion.)
  • The Lorentz Factor: γ = 1 / cos(α) = 1 / √(1 - sin²(α)) = 1 / √(1 - (v_spatial/c)²_
    (The Lorentz factor, derived geometrically from the temporal angle α.)

The Macro-Micro Bridge[cite: 4]:

This hypothesized equation links the macroscopic, relativistic motion (α) to the microscopic, internal quantum state of the gyroscope (its internal spin ωX), bridging the theory of scale:

ωX = (m₀ * c² * tan(α)) / (k * h)

(Where m₀ is rest mass, h is Planck's constant, and k is a dimensionless factor.)

5. Other Emergent Phenomena (Summary)

  • Forces: Are thermodynamic mediators that apply "FEN torques" to the temporo-gyroscopes, altering their spin states (and thus their motion, mass, or identity) to resolve TDF disequilibrium[cite: 3].
  • Cosmic Expansion: Arises from the collective, large-scale divergence of the unique, individual temporal axes of all unbound matter[cite: 1, 2, 3].
  • The Constant G (L³ / MT²): The dimensional structure of G is interpreted as a fundamental ratio: the emergent, "relatable" 3D spatial geometry (L³) divided by the "unrelatable," fundamental temporal machinery (Mass M as temporal volume, and T² as the temporal processing rate at the Objective Now)[cite: 3].
